# Tenant control is not sovereignty

Satya Nadella has [named the trap](https://snscratchpad.com/posts/reverse-information-paradox/). With AI you pay for intelligence twice. Once in money, and again in the proprietary knowledge you have to reveal to make that intelligence useful. Arrow's old paradox was the seller's problem, that you cannot sell information without giving it away. This is the buyer's version. The model learns from your exhaust, the prompts, the tool calls, and above all the corrections you make when it is wrong, and that learning flows one way, toward whoever owns the infrastructure. He is right. It is the clearest statement of the problem I have seen from inside the industry.

Then notice who is saying it. The person describing how value converges on the owner of the learning infrastructure owns the largest learning infrastructure there is. And the fix he offers is a private environment inside his own cloud. Name the fear, sell the cure. The cure is a tenant.

A tenant is a promise, not a property. Retaining control of your data inside someone else's cloud is a contract, and a contract can be re-priced, re-termed or revoked. It sits on hardware you do not own, under a jurisdiction you did not choose, and what the provider learns from you comes down in the end to trust. Alex Karp put the harder question [plainly](https://x.com/PalantirTech/status/2072326189079757277): if it were so valuable, why sell it by the token, why not take a share of what it makes you? What technical customers actually want is to own the means of production. Their compute, their models, their data, their edge.

We would go one step past the tenant. The only defence against having your knowledge extracted is an origin you own, can inspect, and can walk away from. Not privacy as a setting you toggle, but privacy as a property of how the thing is built. When leaving is always possible the extraction cannot happen, because the moment it starts you are already gone. That is not a feature. It is the architecture.

Nadella's own remedy has the right shape once you take it seriously. Human capital and token capital compound, he says, in a learning loop you own. But a loop you own needs a substrate you own. The corrections, the evaluations, the institutional know-how, the canonical record of what your organisation knows and can do, all of it has to live somewhere that is yours, portable and legible, not scattered as exhaust across a provider you rent. That substrate has a name. It is an origin.

You pay for AI twice only if you rent it. Own the origin, and you pay once.
