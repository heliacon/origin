01 · Foundations

The two laws.

Everything in the system is downstream of two rules. Hold these and the rest follows. Break them and no amount of polish will save it.

Law one

Colour only ever means something.

The interface is warm neutral ink on warm paper, or the reverse at night. Colour is rationed. Prussian blue is the human signature, the thing to act on. Brass is the machine, data and signal. Green, amber and red are status only. If a colour is not carrying one of those meanings, it is wrong.

Law two

Every claim carries its provenance.

Heliacon sells trust, so the design leaves room for the receipt. A source, a version, a date, an answer that cites where it came from, all set in the machine voice. Nothing on the surface asserts what it cannot trace. Provenance is structural, not a footnote.

04 · Foundations

One serif reads, a sans labels, a monospace speaks for the machine.

EB Garamond carries body and display, the way a book does: a true old-style, light on the page, made to be read for a thousand words without tiring. Plex Sans is the neutral interface workhorse. Plex Mono is the machine voice.

The reading face, at length

On being the first light

The web spent thirty years assuming a reader with a screen and a scroll. That reader is still here, but they are no longer alone. The next thing to read your work is as likely to be a model assembling an answer as a person reading a page, and the model has no patience for layout. It wants the source, and it wants to know where the source came from. So we build from the origin: one canonical record of what a company knows and can do, published in whatever form the reader can use, each carrying its own provenance. When a machine assembles an answer from a dozen sources, the source that can be checked is worth more than the source that ranks first, because ranking is rented and provenance is owned.

07 · Brand & structure

The mark rises once.

The wordmark is fixed. The O is the sun, a compass, first light, and it is the only place the human accent is allowed to sit in the mark. Everywhere else the letters are ink. The shape never changes; only its colour answers to the theme.

Lockups · three grounds, one behaviour

Primary · ink letters, Prussian O, on paper
Reversed · single cream on ink
On accent · single tone on Prussian

Two signals never appear in the mark at once. On any coloured ground the wordmark goes to a single tone, so the O reads as a letter, not a second accent competing with the field it sits on.

The mark alone · reserved use

Avatar, favicon, provenance stamp only
App icon · rounded square, 22% radius

Clear space & minimum size

≥ 132px · full wordmark
≥ 24px · mark only

Keep clear space of at least the height of the O on every side. Below 132px the wordmark loses the O detail, so drop to the mark alone.

Never

✕ Skew, rotate or stretch
✕ Place on a busy image
✕ Recolour to anything but ink or accent
✕ Tint the mark brass or a status colour

10 · Brand & structure

The image is atmosphere.

Imagery is cartographic and elemental: light over terrain, survey over land, the moment before a thing is charted. It sets a mood under the content, it never carries the message. Photographs are washed toward Prussian so the whole site reads as one atlas, and the words always ride above in glass.

Treatment · from source to signature

01 · source frame, as shot
02 · washed toward Prussian
03 · lower edge dissolved to paper
Origin architecture
04 · content in glass above

Do

  • Keep one cool cast across every image so pages feel like one atlas
  • Dissolve the lower edge into paper so the sheet floats free
  • Leave the quiet side of the frame for the type to sit on
  • Treat the image as ground, the words as the figure

Don't

  • Set headings inside the photograph and end the image with a hard seam
  • Let an image run warm or saturated enough to fight the accent
  • Stack text straight on a busy frame with no glass between
  • Use imagery where a plain paper ground would read clearer

14 · Components

Built to hold a thousand words.

The journal is where the type earns its keep. One measure, generous leading, Garamond at reading size. Every element a long piece needs is here and nothing it does not: heading, quote, pull quote, code, figure and the cited footnote.

Some of what I learnt building my own stack

Journal · 12 min · 2026 · 05

For a year I ran the whole thing on one machine. No cloud, no gateway I did not own, no answer I could not trace. What follows is the part that surprised me, set down before I forget the shape of it.

The measure matters more than the font

A good face set too wide reads worse than a plain one set right. Sixty-odd characters to the line, leading opened past what feels natural on screen, and the words start to behave like a book rather than a feed.

Local is not private. Private is a property you have to engineer, and the model running on your own silicon is only the first inch of it.

The gateway logs are content blind by design. You can read that the request happened 1 without reading what was in it, which is the only honest way to keep a ledger.

$ forge -f notes.md "summarise as bullets" --small
  → routed: local-4b · 0 tokens to cloud

The test is simple. Would I rewrite this anyway, and is a mediocre version cheap? Then it goes local.

1 Gateway ledger, entry class request.meta, retained ninety days, content excluded.

16 · Quality & reference

Plain, precise, British.

Heliacon writes the way it builds: no slop, no salesmanship, every claim able to carry its own weight. Short sentences. British spelling. The reader is treated as clever and busy. If a line could appear in any startup's copy, it is wrong.

Say

One source of truth, projected into every surface.

The gateway is content blind by design.

Exit rights, not good intentions.

Nothing on the surface asserts what it cannot trace.

Not

Revolutionary AI-powered synergy for the modern web.

We take your privacy incredibly seriously.

Trust us, your data is safe.

Leverage best-in-class solutions to unlock value.

Spelling British English throughout. Colour, not color.
Punctuation No Oxford comma. No em dash, a hyphen does the work.
Claims Every claim traceable, or it does not ship.
Length Short over long. Cut the sentence that repeats.
Person Direct. The reader is clever and busy.
Names Machine things in the machine voice, mono and exact.
