# Heliacon positioning brief

Working brief, not site copy. Where Heliacon plants its flag and why.

## The house line

**Tenant control is not sovereignty. Only exit is.**

The only real defence against having your knowledge extracted is an origin you own, can inspect, and can walk away from. Privacy and control as a property of the architecture, not a promise in a contract.

## The air cover (2026-07-12)

Satya Nadella named our category from the top of the industry in *The Reverse Information Paradox* (snscratchpad.com, his personal blog; corroborated by Business Standard and his LinkedIn). The argument: with AI you pay twice, once in money and once in the proprietary knowledge you must reveal; models learn from your "exhaust" (prompts, tool calls, corrections, evals); value converges on the owner of the learning infrastructure, not the creator of the knowledge. Alex Karp (Palantir) says the sharper version: own the means of production, "why are they charging for tokens if it's so valuable?"

**Use it. When the Microsoft CEO publicly argues that you must retain control of your knowledge and avoid single-provider dependence, the ground Heliacon stands on is now mainstream.** "Even Nadella says you're paying twice" is a legitimate opener.

## The map — his four C's are our architecture

| Nadella's C | Heliacon / Topkay |
|---|---|
| **Control** (own your memory, traces, evals) | Own your **origin** — the canonical record of what you know and can do |
| **Choice** (decouple from any single model) | Model-agnostic, portable; the twin inspectable from every consumer viewpoint; no lock-in |
| **Capability** (private learning environment) | Build the learning loop on a substrate you own |
| **Cost** (efficient allocation) | Efficiency of an owned, deterministic origin over rented per-token exhaust |

"Human capital + token capital compound in a loop you own" = an argument for an owned, improving origin. The origin is the substrate of the loop.

## The differentiation (where we are NOT them)

- **vs Nadella / Azure:** his fix is a tenant boundary *inside his own cloud* — diagnosis-as-marketing from the largest infrastructure owner. A tenant is a contract (re-priceable, revocable, someone else's hardware and jurisdiction); sovereignty is a property of how the thing is built. We are the architecture-not-policy version.
- **vs enterprise-only framing:** his remedy (private RL environments) is a big-co capability. We democratise the same principle to any size, including individuals who own the hardware and run local. This is the [[project_private_data_agent|local-AI]] thesis applied to the firm.
- **vs Karp:** aligned — he is the honest edge of the same argument. Cite him, don't compete with him.

## Watch-outs

- Be fair to Nadella — his five principles are sensible; the critique is the conflict of interest and tenant≠sovereignty, not that he is wrong. Steelman before you counter.
- Do not overclaim. Exit rights stop the *extractive* capture; they do not make software improve on its own. Precision survives a technical audience; slogans do not.
- Heliacon must clear its own bar: any product that adds a subscription, a cloud dependency or a proprietary format makes this line a hostage to fortune. The principle is a forcing function on us first.
