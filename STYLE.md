# Heliacon style

How everything here is written. These are rules, not suggestions.

- British English. Organise, colour, licence.
- No Oxford comma. "knowledge, capability and provenance".
- No em dash. Use a hyphen, or split into two sentences. Never the long dash.
- Semicolons rarely. A full stop is usually better.
- No brackets. Rewrite the sentence instead.
- Rarely hyphenate compounds. Rarely put a word in quotes.
- No slop. No filler, no puff, no throat-clearing. Short declaratives. Receipts over claims.

Everything is written to stand on its own. The corpus does not reference products, sites or
tools that are not yet live. When it can name a thing, it names the thing. Until then it speaks
for itself.
